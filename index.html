“HELLO”: A Simple Word That Can Change Everything"


Short Film:

This blog shares insights and reflections on the award-winning short film HELLO (1st Place in the Nikon Cinema Z Film Fest 2019) directed by Richard Galapate. Through the reactions of different viewers, it highlights how a simple word like “hello” can break loneliness, build connection, and spread kindness. It reminds us that small acts of compassion can make a big difference in someone’s life and that understanding and acceptance often begin with one small gesture.

INSIGHTS:

The short film HELLO, directed by Richard Galapate, and winner of 1st Place in the Nikon Cinema Z Film Fest 2019, shares a heartfelt message about kindness, acceptance, and connection. It imagines a world where a simple “hello” could be the antidote to loneliness and the bridge to unity among different kinds of people. In a time when everyone is connected online but still feels emotionally distant, the film reminds us that even a small gesture of kindness can have a big impact.

The story follows Charles, a loner who likes being alone but hates the feeling of loneliness. He spends most of his time in front of his computer and avoids social interaction because he’s afraid to reach out. Everything changes when a woman named Angelica says a simple “hello.” That single word becomes the start of change, proving that kindness and connection can start with even the smallest act.

As Relox wrote, the film captures the idea that reaching out, no matter how small the effort, can lead to understanding, acceptance, and friendship. Meanwhile, Cisneros reflected that the story teaches how fear can stop us from growing and connecting. He realized that a simple “hello” or small act of kindness can open doors to new relationships and that embracing diversity allows deeper connections to grow.

Borbe’s reflection focused on how the film reminds us that kindness doesn’t need to be big or expensive—it can start with something as easy as a smile or greeting. She was touched by how the movie showed people from different backgrounds and situations, teaching that no matter how different we are, everyone deserves kindness and respect.

In the end, all three viewers agreed that HELLO is more than just a short film—it’s a gentle reminder to be kind, open, and understanding. It teaches that compassion and connection begin with simple efforts and that even one word can make someone feel seen and valued. The film shows us that sometimes, the courage to say “hello” can truly make a difference.
